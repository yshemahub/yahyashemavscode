About

The Bell Affair is an animated feature film centered on the true story of Daniel and Mary Bell's legal fight for freedom from slavery and their subsequent large-scale escape attempt in American history. Production took place entirely remotely during COVID-19 — the cast and director were never physically in the same room.

Research

Background

Salt Marsh Production Studio acquired motion capture suits in 2021. This research aimed to integrate the technology into The Bell Affair's post-production pipeline and identify applications across the studio's other projects.

Research Goals

  • Seamlessly integrate motion capture into The Bell Affair's animation pipeline.
  • Identify opportunities for scaling the technology across other studio projects.

Methodology

The research employed case studies and controlled experiments. The initial focus was assessing motion-capture retargeting techniques from Rokoko to Daz 3D MetaHuman characters. Subsequent case studies applied the resulting workflows across various studio projects to determine the optimal use cases.

Key Insights

  1. A-pose to T-pose transfer issues. Direct motion transfer between Rokoko's T-pose rig and Daz MetaHuman's A-pose rig creates significant error rates due to the pose difference.
  2. Selective workflow efficiency. Motion capture improved efficiency in specific animation shots, though the benefit diminished in sequences with minimal full-body movement.
  3. Unreal Engine performance. Unreal Engine and its MetaHuman characters performed exceptionally well — better than alternatives for diverse character creation, photorealistic output, comprehensive filmmaking capability, and community support.
  4. Motion capture advantages. The technology proved most beneficial for full-body movement sequences.

Recommendations

  1. Rig pose adjustment. Use Blender or Maya to convert Daz rigs to T-pose before retargeting — this significantly improves transfer accuracy.
  2. Pre-set animation libraries. Save animations in Daz Studio preset format for convenient reuse, particularly valuable since most Bell Affair scenes didn't require motion capture.
  3. Unreal Engine integration. Use Unreal Engine MetaHumans with motion capture for future projects, given the feature diversity, minimal error rates, free licensing, and strong community resources.
